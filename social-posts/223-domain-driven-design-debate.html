DDD Is Over-Applied by Teams Who Don't Need It

DDD is over-applied by teams who don't need it. Bounded contexts, aggregates, value objects, domain events — for a team of 5 building a CRUD app, this is architecture astronautics. DDD shines for complex domains, not simple ones.

The Real Trade-offs

  • DDD is a thinking tool, not a coding framework: The most valuable part of DDD is the strategic patterns — ubiquitous language, bounded contexts, context maps. The tactical patterns (entities, repositories) are less critical.
  • Small teams cannot afford DDD overhead: Event storming workshops, domain experts, aggregate design — a 3-person startup iterating weekly does not have time for this. Ship first, refactor to DDD when complexity demands it.
  • Bounded contexts map perfectly to microservices: For large organizations with complex domains, DDD bounded contexts define natural service boundaries. Without DDD, microservice boundaries are arbitrary and wrong.
  • Aggregate design prevents distributed transactions: Properly designed aggregates ensure all invariants are checked within a single transaction. Cross-aggregate operations use domain events — eliminating distributed transactions.
  • Most "DDD" implementations are just folder structures: Creating folders named Domain, Application, Infrastructure is not DDD. Without ubiquitous language and deep domain modeling, it is just Clean Architecture with extra steps.
