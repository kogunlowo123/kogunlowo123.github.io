Microservices Are Over-Engineered for 90% of Teams

Your team of 8 engineers doesn't need 47 microservices, a service mesh, and a distributed tracing platform. A well-structured monolith deploys faster, costs less, and actually ships features. The microservices hype has created more legacy disasters than it solved.

The Real Trade-offs

  • Monoliths on ECS Fargate scale horizontally just fine — you don't need microservices for scaling
  • Distributed tracing with AWS X-Ray wouldn't be needed if you didn't split your app into 30 pieces
  • A single Aurora database with read replicas handles more traffic than most startups will ever see
  • EKS costs $73/month for the control plane alone — before a single pod runs
  • Microservices require SQS, SNS, and eventual consistency patterns most teams can't debug
