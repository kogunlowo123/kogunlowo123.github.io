The Real Trade-offs

  • VPC Peering is free for data: Peering has no per-GB data processing charge (only standard cross-AZ/region transfer). For high-traffic VPC pairs, peering saves thousands per month compared to Transit Gateway.
  • Hybrid approach is optimal: Use Transit Gateway for VPN/Direct Connect termination and low-traffic VPCs. Use VPC Peering for high-traffic VPC pairs. This hybrid model gives you hub-and-spoke simplicity where needed and cost efficiency where it matters.
  • Attachment costs add up too: Each VPC attachment costs $0.05/hour (~$36/month). 20 VPCs = $720/month just for attachments, before any data flows.
  • Cross-region peering through TGW is double-taxed: Inter-region Transit Gateway peering charges data processing on both sides plus inter-region transfer. The bill compounds fast.
  • The management benefit is real: Transit Gateway simplifies routing tables, centralizes network management, and enables network-level segmentation. For 50+ VPCs, the operational savings may justify the data costs.
