The Real Trade-offs

  • Data transfer savings alone justify it: DX egress rates are $0.02/GB vs $0.09/GB over internet. Transfer 10TB/month and DX saves $700/month in egress alone — paying for the port fee and cross-connect within weeks.
  • Consistent latency matters: VPN latency varies with internet congestion. DX provides consistent sub-millisecond jitter. For database replication, real-time analytics, and financial workloads, this consistency is non-negotiable.
  • The lead time is painful: DX provisioning takes 2-12 weeks depending on location and provider. You can't spin it up in an emergency. VPN is instant. Smart teams use VPN as backup for DX — not the other way around.
  • Hosted connections lower the bar: You don't need a dedicated 1 Gbps port. AWS Partners offer hosted connections from 50 Mbps to 10 Gbps. Start small, grow as needed.
  • Redundancy requires two connections: A single DX connection is a single point of failure. AWS recommends two connections at different locations. That doubles the cost but is essential for production workloads.
