Pilot Light vs Warm Standby: Where's the Break-Even Point?

If your hourly downtime cost exceeds $50K, Warm Standby pays for itself within a single incident. Below that threshold, Pilot Light with solid IaC automation gives you 80% of the recovery speed at 20% of the cost. Do the math before choosing.

The Real Trade-offs

  • Pilot Light costs ~5-15% of production spend: Only data replication and core infrastructure definitions stay active. Compute, load balancers, and application tiers are provisioned on-demand during failover via IaC.
  • Warm Standby costs ~30-50% of production spend: A scaled-down but functional copy of your entire stack runs 24/7. You pay for idle compute, networking, and load balancers continuously.
  • The break-even formula is straightforward: (Warm Standby monthly cost - Pilot Light monthly cost) vs (hourly downtime cost x additional recovery hours). If one extra hour of downtime costs more than the monthly delta, Warm Standby wins.
  • Pilot Light's hidden risk is configuration drift: Since compute isn't running, you won't discover broken AMIs, missing IAM permissions, or changed API endpoints until you actually failover. Warm Standby catches these issues proactively.
  • Counter-argument — modern IaC eliminates Pilot Light risks: With Terraform, tested CloudFormation stacks, and container images in ECR, Pilot Light recovery is deterministic. The "untested infrastructure" argument is an automation maturity problem, not an architecture problem.
