The Real Trade-offs

  • Cloudflare wins on speed: Cloudflare operates 300+ PoPs globally with sub-10ms average resolution. Route 53 is fast but Cloudflare's anycast network is consistently ranked #1 in DNS speed benchmarks.
  • Route 53 wins on integration: Alias records for ALB, CloudFront, S3, and API Gateway. Private hosted zones for VPC DNS. Route 53 Resolver for hybrid DNS. None of this works with Cloudflare.
  • Private DNS is Route 53 only: If you need internal service discovery, split-horizon DNS, or VPC-associated DNS, Route 53 is your only native option. Cloudflare doesn't operate inside your VPC.
  • Cost comparison at scale: 100 hosted zones on Route 53 = $50/month minimum. 10M queries/month adds $4. On Cloudflare, all of that is free. For public-facing DNS, the savings are real.
  • Hybrid approach is optimal: Use Cloudflare for public DNS (faster, cheaper) and Route 53 for private DNS and AWS-integrated records (Alias records, health checks). Best of both worlds.
