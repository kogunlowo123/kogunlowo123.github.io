Eventual Consistency Means Eventual Bugs

Eventual consistency means eventual bugs. When your order service says "confirmed" but your inventory service still shows the item as available, a second customer buys the same item. Congratulations, you now have a consistency problem disguised as a feature.

The Real Trade-offs

  • Users experience consistency bugs as real problems: "I just updated my profile but the old name still shows" — that is eventual consistency visible to users. It erodes trust even when technically correct.
  • DynamoDB strong reads cost 2x: Strongly consistent reads consume twice the read capacity of eventually consistent reads. For high-throughput tables, that doubles your read cost.
  • Sagas are eventual consistency with guardrails: The saga pattern accepts eventual consistency but adds compensating transactions. If step 3 fails, undo steps 1 and 2. Complex but necessary for distributed transactions.
  • Read-your-own-writes is the minimum bar: Even in eventually consistent systems, users should always see their own recent changes. Route reads to the writer node or use session consistency.
  • Most teams do not need eventual consistency: If you are not operating at massive scale across regions, a single Aurora cluster with strong consistency is simpler, cheaper, and correct by default.
