Lambda vs Fargate vs EC2: The Real Cost Debate

Lambda is cheaper only if you ignore developer time. When you factor in cold starts, payload limits, execution timeouts, and the contortions your code goes through to fit serverless constraints — Fargate or even EC2 often wins on total cost of ownership.

The Real Trade-offs

  • Lambda looks cheap on paper: $0.20 per 1M requests is enticing, but factor in API Gateway costs, CloudWatch logs, cold start workarounds, and the 15-minute timeout forcing architectural gymnastics — the bill adds up fast.
  • Fargate is the middle ground most ignore: No server management, no cold starts, and you can run any container. At steady-state traffic, Fargate with Savings Plans often beats Lambda on cost.
  • EC2 with Reserved Instances is still king for predictable workloads: If your traffic is consistent, a reserved t3.medium costs ~$30/month. Try running Lambda at that rate with equivalent compute — you can't.
  • Developer productivity is the hidden cost: Lambda forces you into smaller functions, event-driven patterns, and specialized debugging tools. For teams not already serverless-native, the ramp-up time is real money.
  • The crossover point matters: Below ~1M requests/month, Lambda wins. Above ~10M steady requests, Fargate or EC2 wins. The messy middle is where most teams live and overpay.
