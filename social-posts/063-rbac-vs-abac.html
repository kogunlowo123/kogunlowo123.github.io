The Real Trade-offs

  • RBAC is universally understood: Every engineer knows roles and permissions. ABAC requires understanding policy languages, attribute evaluation, and context propagation. Complexity = misconfiguration risk.
  • Role explosion is real: A 500-person org with fine-grained RBAC ends up with 2,000+ roles. Each new feature creates new roles. ABAC eliminates this with attribute-based conditions.
  • AWS already does ABAC: IAM policy conditions based on tags, IP ranges, time, and MFA status are ABAC. Most teams use it without realizing it. The hybrid model works.
  • ABAC is harder to audit: "Who has access to what?" is simple with RBAC — check role assignments. With ABAC, access depends on runtime context. Auditors hate it.
  • The future is Relationship-Based (ReBAC): Google Zanzibar, SpiceDB, and OpenFGA model access as relationships. Neither RBAC nor ABAC handles "can edit if author or team member" as elegantly.
