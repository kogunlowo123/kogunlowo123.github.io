The Real Trade-offs

  • Cross-region data transfer costs add up fast: At $0.02/GB for inter-region transfer, a workload pushing 10TB/month pays $200/month just in replication costs. For petabyte-scale data lakes, this becomes a six-figure annual expense.
  • Replication lag is unavoidable: Asynchronous cross-region replication typically has 100ms-2s lag. For financial transactions or inventory systems, this means your DR copy is always slightly behind — your RPO is never truly zero.
  • Multi-AZ is sufficient for most failure modes: The vast majority of outages are single-AZ failures, not full region failures. Multi-AZ deployments cover 99% of incidents at a fraction of cross-region cost.
  • Counter-argument — region-level outages do happen: When us-east-1 goes down, every Multi-AZ deployment in that region goes with it. The question isn't "will it happen" but "can your business survive 4-8 hours of downtime?"
  • Tiered replication is the pragmatic approach: Replicate tier-1 critical data (customer records, transactions) cross-region. Keep tier-2 data (logs, analytics) in a single region with cross-region backups on a daily schedule.
