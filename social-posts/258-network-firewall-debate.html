AWS Network Firewall Is Overpriced Suricata

AWS took open-source Suricata, wrapped it in a managed service, and charges $0.395/hr per endpoint plus $0.065/GB. You could run Suricata on a c6g.large for $0.068/hr and get the same IPS/IDS rules. Is the "managed" tax worth 6x the price?

The Real Trade-offs

  • The managed tax is real: Network Firewall in 3 AZs costs ~$854/month just for endpoints. Add data processing at $0.065/GB and 10TB/month becomes $1,504/month. That's $2,358/month before you inspect a single packet.
  • But self-managed means self-patched: Running your own Suricata means managing HA, auto-scaling, patching, rule updates, and monitoring. One missed CVE and your "savings" become a breach. Most teams can't staff this.
  • Security Groups and NACLs are free: For 90% of use cases, Security Groups (stateful L4) and NACLs (stateless L3/L4) provide sufficient network filtering. You don't need deep packet inspection for every workload.
  • Compliance drives the decision: PCI-DSS, HIPAA, and FedRAMP often require IDS/IPS capabilities. AWS Network Firewall checks that compliance box with a managed service that auditors understand.
  • Third-party alternatives exist: Palo Alto, Fortinet, and Check Point all offer VM-based firewalls on AWS Marketplace. They often cost less than Network Firewall while providing more features — but add vendor lock-in.
