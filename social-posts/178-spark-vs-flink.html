Spark vs Flink — The Engine War That Defines Your Data Stack

Spark has the ecosystem, the community, and the Databricks money machine. Flink has true event-time processing, lower latency, and stateful streaming that Spark's micro-batch model can't match. But here's the thing — 80% of companies picking Flink would be better served by Spark Structured Streaming. The remaining 20% absolutely cannot live without Flink.

The Real Trade-offs

  • Spark's ecosystem is unmatched: MLlib, GraphX, Spark SQL, Delta Lake integration — Spark is a platform, not just a processing engine. Finding Spark developers is 10x easier than finding Flink experts. This talent gap matters more than benchmarks.
  • Flink's streaming model is genuinely superior: Native event-time processing, exactly-once state management, and savepoints for job versioning. For complex event processing (CEP), Flink's programming model is fundamentally better designed.
  • State management is the hidden differentiator: Flink manages terabytes of state with RocksDB backends and incremental checkpointing. Spark Structured Streaming state handling works but hits scaling limits for windowed aggregations over large key spaces.
  • The convergence is real: Spark is getting faster streaming (Project Lightspeed), Flink is improving batch (Flink SQL). In 2-3 years, the gap narrows significantly. Pick based on today's team skills, not future roadmaps.
  • Managed services change the calculus: Databricks makes Spark turnkey. Amazon Managed Flink and Confluent Cloud reduce Flink's operational burden. The "hard to operate" argument weakens when you're not managing clusters yourself.
