CMMC Level 2 requires full implementation of 110 NIST SP 800-171 controls with third-party assessment. A 50-person defense subcontractor spending $200K-500K on compliance infrastructure cannot compete with primes who amortize the cost across billions in contracts.

The Real Trade-offs

  • Compliance cost is a fixed overhead: Whether you're a $5M or $5B contractor, CMMC Level 2 costs roughly the same to implement. Small businesses absorb a disproportionate percentage of revenue on compliance.
  • Cloud services reduce the burden: AWS GovCloud and Azure Government provide inherently compliant infrastructure, reducing the number of controls small contractors need to implement themselves.
  • CUI scoping is the real challenge: Most contractors don't know where CUI lives in their systems. Data discovery and classification is a prerequisite that many underestimate by months.
  • The DIB supply chain needs protection: Nation-state actors target small subcontractors as entry points to defense systems. CMMC raises the bar where self-attestation clearly failed.
  • Counter-argument — compliance doesn't equal security: Checking 110 control boxes doesn't prevent breaches. Motivated attackers bypass compliant systems regularly. The focus should be on outcomes, not controls.
