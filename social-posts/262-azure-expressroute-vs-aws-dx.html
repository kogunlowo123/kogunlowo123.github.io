ExpressRoute Is Better Designed Than Direct Connect

ExpressRoute gives you Microsoft peering (Office 365 + Azure public services) and private peering on one circuit. Direct Connect makes you manage separate VIFs. ExpressRoute Global Reach connects sites through Microsoft's backbone. AWS has no equivalent.

The Real Trade-offs

  • ExpressRoute's peering model is cleaner: One circuit supports private peering (VNets) and Microsoft peering (O365, Azure PaaS) simultaneously. DX requires separate VIFs and can't natively reach AWS public services without a public VIF.
  • Global Reach is a game-changer: Connect two on-premises sites through Microsoft's backbone without deploying WAN infrastructure. AWS has no equivalent — you'd need third-party SD-WAN or MPLS.
  • DX has more location flexibility: AWS Direct Connect is available in more colocation facilities worldwide. ExpressRoute locations are more limited, especially outside major metros.
  • Pricing models differ significantly: ExpressRoute offers unlimited data plans (metered or unlimited). DX charges per-port-hour plus egress. For predictable high-bandwidth workloads, ExpressRoute's unlimited plan can be significantly cheaper.
  • Redundancy design differs: ExpressRoute circuits include built-in redundancy (two connections per circuit). DX requires you to explicitly provision two connections for HA. ExpressRoute's model is simpler to reason about.
