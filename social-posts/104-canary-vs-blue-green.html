Blue-Green Deployments Are Wasteful — Canary Wins

Blue-green deployments require maintaining two full production environments. That's 2x your infrastructure cost for the privilege of a fast rollback. Canary deployments give you the same safety at 1-5% extra cost, with better signal on real user behavior.

The Real Trade-offs

  • Blue-green is simpler to reason about: You have green (live) and blue (new). Swap the load balancer. Done. No traffic splitting, no metric comparison. Simplicity has value.
  • Canary gives real user signal: 5% of real traffic hitting the new version catches issues that staging never will. You test with actual user behavior, not synthetic tests.
  • Database migrations break blue-green: If your schema changed, you can't just swap back. Both strategies struggle with stateful changes — canary at least limits blast radius.
  • Cost is a real factor: Blue-green doubles your compute during deployment windows. For large-scale services, this can be massive. Canary adds incremental capacity.
  • Canary requires observability maturity: You need good metrics, automated analysis, and clear SLIs to run canary well. Blue-green just needs a health check.
