The Real Trade-offs

  • Complexity breeds misconfiguration: Conflicting CA policies can lock out admins, break service principals, or create gaps. Testing CA policies in report-only mode is essential but rarely done.
  • Device compliance is the game-changer: Requiring managed, compliant devices eliminates BYOD risk entirely. But it also means contractors, vendors, and personal devices are blocked — which impacts flexibility.
  • Risk-based policies are underrated: Identity Protection can detect impossible travel, anonymous IPs, and password spray attacks — then automatically require step-up authentication. This is Zero Trust in action.
  • CA policies require P1/P2 licenses: Basic CA requires Entra ID P1. Risk-based policies require P2. For organizations on free Entra ID, this entire capability is gated behind licensing.
  • Service principals need CA too: Workload identity CA policies are newer but critical. An automated service account without conditional access is an unprotected backdoor.
