The Real Trade-offs

  • Enforcement requires executive buy-in: Freezing feature deployments when the error budget is exhausted means telling product "no." Without VP-level sponsorship, SRE teams can't enforce this.
  • Error budget policies need teeth: Google's model includes escalation paths — reduced deployment velocity, mandatory reliability sprints, and leadership reviews. Half-measures don't work.
  • SLOs must reflect real user pain: A 99.9% SLO on a service that handles 100 requests/day means you tolerate 0.1 errors/day. That's meaningless. SLOs must be calibrated to actual traffic and impact.
  • The cultural shift is harder than the tooling: Prometheus and Grafana can track burn rates. The hard part is getting product managers to accept that "we can't ship because we're unreliable."
  • Error budgets align incentives: When done right, developers care about reliability because blowing the budget means they can't ship. It turns reliability from an ops problem into everyone's problem.
