The Real Trade-offs

  • DORA metrics are directionally correct: The research behind DORA is solid — high-performing teams do deploy more frequently with lower failure rates. The problem is using them as management KPIs instead of engineering diagnostics.
  • Gaming is inevitable when metrics meet management: "Our deployment frequency went up 400%!" Yeah, because you split one release into 4 deploys. The outcome didn't change — only the metric did.
  • Change failure rate is subjective: What counts as a "failure"? A rollback? A hotfix? A degraded experience? Teams that define failure narrowly get better numbers. Teams that define it honestly get better systems.
  • Use metrics for trends, not comparisons: DORA metrics are useful for tracking your own improvement over time. They're dangerous when used to compare teams or justify promotions.
  • Developer satisfaction is the missing metric: DORA added "reliability" as a 5th metric in recent research. But developer experience and satisfaction might matter more than any pipeline metric.
