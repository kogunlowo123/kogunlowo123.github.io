The Real Trade-offs

  • AI catches consistency issues perfectly: Naming conventions, missing error handling, unused imports — AI never gets bored reviewing the same patterns across 50 PRs a day.
  • Humans catch design smells: "This works but couples two services that should be independent" — that insight requires understanding the system's evolution and business roadmap.
  • AI reviews are instant: Feedback in seconds vs. hours waiting for a human reviewer. This alone makes AI review worth adopting for initial passes.
  • False positive fatigue is real: AI reviewers that flag too many non-issues train developers to ignore all AI feedback, defeating the purpose.
  • The hybrid model wins: AI handles style, security patterns, and common bugs. Humans focus on architecture, maintainability, and business logic correctness.
