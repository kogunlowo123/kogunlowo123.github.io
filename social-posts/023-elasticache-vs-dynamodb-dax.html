Redis Is the Most Over-Used Service on AWS

Every team adds ElastiCache Redis "for caching." But half the time, DynamoDB DAX, CloudFront caching, or even application-level caching would be simpler and cheaper. Redis becomes a session store, a queue, a pub/sub system, and a cache — then it becomes your single point of failure.

The Real Trade-offs

  • ElastiCache Redis costs $0.068/hr minimum — that's $50/month for a cache.t3.micro with no HA
  • DAX is a transparent cache for DynamoDB — no cache invalidation logic needed
  • HTTP caching with CloudFront headers is free and handles most read-heavy patterns
  • Using Redis as a queue? SQS is more reliable and costs $0.40 per million requests
  • Redis is perfect for leaderboards, rate limiting, and real-time analytics — not as a general-purpose cache
