SRE and DevOps Are the Same Role with Different Salaries

Google says "SRE implements DevOps." LinkedIn says SREs earn 20-30% more. Most SRE job descriptions are copy-pasted DevOps descriptions with "SLO" and "error budget" added. Are we just rebranding ops engineers with fancier titles to justify higher comp?

The Real Trade-offs

  • SRE has a prescriptive framework: Google's SRE book defines specific practices — error budgets, SLOs, toil budgets, blameless postmortems. DevOps is a culture movement without a concrete implementation.
  • DevOps is broader: DevOps covers CI/CD, IaC, culture change, and breaking down silos. SRE focuses specifically on reliability. DevOps is the philosophy; SRE is one implementation.
  • The salary gap is real but misleading: SRE roles often require stronger software engineering skills — writing production code, not just scripts. The higher pay reflects deeper engineering expectations.
  • Most companies do neither well: They hire "SRE" or "DevOps" engineers who end up being ops engineers managing Jenkins and writing Bash scripts. The title doesn't matter if the practices don't follow.
  • Platform engineering is the new rebranding: Now companies are creating "Platform Engineer" roles that look suspiciously like SRE which looked suspiciously like DevOps. The cycle continues.
