The Real Trade-offs

  • CMKs enable cross-account sharing: AWS-managed keys can't be shared across accounts. For multi-account architectures, CMKs with cross-account grants are the only option.
  • Key policy auditing is valuable: CMK key policies let you track exactly who can decrypt what data. AWS-managed keys use opaque service-linked policies you can't customize.
  • Deletion risk is catastrophic: Accidentally deleting or disabling a CMK makes all data encrypted with it permanently inaccessible. AWS has a 7-30 day waiting period, but mistakes still happen.
  • Regulatory requirements may mandate CMKs: Some compliance frameworks require customer-controlled encryption keys with documented key management procedures. AWS-managed keys don't satisfy this.
  • Cost adds up at scale: CMKs cost $1/month each plus $0.03 per 10,000 API calls. With hundreds of keys across multiple regions and accounts, costs become non-trivial.
