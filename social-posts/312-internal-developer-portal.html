An Internal Developer Portal centralizes it all — but is the investment worth it at that size, or is this Spotify-scale thinking applied too early?

The Real Trade-offs

  • 50 engineers is the tipping point: Below 50, everyone knows everyone and tribal knowledge works. Above 50, service discovery, ownership, and onboarding become real bottlenecks.
  • Backstage is powerful but complex: Running Backstage requires a dedicated team. If you can't staff it, consider managed alternatives like Port or Cortex.
  • Self-service templates are the killer feature: "Create a new microservice" as a single button click with opinionated defaults is where IDPs deliver 10x ROI.
  • Stale catalogs are worse than none: An IDP with outdated ownership data and dead links erodes trust. If you can't commit to keeping it current, don't build one.
  • Start small, grow incrementally: Service catalog first, then templates, then docs integration. Don't try to build the full Spotify stack on day one.
