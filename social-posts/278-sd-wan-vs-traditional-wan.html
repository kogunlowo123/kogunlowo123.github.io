SD-WAN vs Traditional WAN: Software-Defined Networking Kills MPLS

MPLS gave us guaranteed SLAs and predictable performance for decades. SD-WAN promises the same quality over cheap broadband and LTE with application-aware routing. The cost savings are 40-70%. But can you really trust the public internet for mission-critical traffic?

The Real Trade-offs

  • Cost savings are real but not universal: Replacing a $1,500/month MPLS circuit with two $200/month broadband links plus SD-WAN licensing saves 60%+ for most branch offices. But headquarters and data centers with 1 Gbps+ needs often still require dedicated circuits.
  • Application-aware routing is the killer feature: SD-WAN can route Zoom calls over the lowest-latency link while sending file backups over the cheapest one — in real time. MPLS treats all packets equally. For cloud-heavy organizations, this intelligence matters more than guaranteed bandwidth.
  • Cloud on-ramps change the economics: SD-WAN vendors like Zscaler, Palo Alto, and VMware have direct peering with AWS, Azure, and GCP. Traffic hits the cloud backbone faster than backhauling through MPLS to a central data center. This is why cloud-first companies abandon MPLS first.
  • SLA guarantees still favor MPLS: MPLS providers guarantee 99.99% uptime and <50ms latency. Internet-based SD-WAN can achieve similar performance 99% of the time but can't contractually guarantee it. Regulated industries often require contractual SLAs.
  • The hybrid approach wins for enterprises: Keep MPLS or Direct Connect for critical sites and real-time traffic. Deploy SD-WAN over broadband for branch offices and non-critical workloads. Phase out MPLS as SD-WAN reliability improves at each site.
