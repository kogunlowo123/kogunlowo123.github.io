The Real Trade-offs

  • v2 scales in increments of 0.5 ACU: Unlike v1's clunky doubling, v2 adds capacity in half-ACU increments in seconds. This fine-grained scaling makes it genuinely useful for variable workloads.
  • The price premium is real: At $0.12/ACU-hour vs ~$0.073/hour for a comparable db.r6g.large, you pay 65% more per unit. At steady state, provisioned Aurora is significantly cheaper.
  • Mixed-mode clusters are the power move: Run your writer as serverless v2 and readers as provisioned instances. Best of both worlds — scaling where it matters, fixed cost where traffic is predictable.
  • v1 trauma is real: Aurora Serverless v1 had 25-second cold starts, no read replicas, and limited engine support. Teams burned by v1 are rightly cautious, even though v2 is a completely different product.
  • Dev/staging environments are the clear win: Serverless v2 for non-production saves 60-80% vs keeping provisioned instances running for environments used 8 hours/day.
