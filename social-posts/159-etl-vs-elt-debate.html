ETL Is Dead — ELT With dbt Is the Only Way

Why transform data before loading it when your warehouse has near-infinite compute? Load raw data, transform with SQL in dbt, version control your models, test your assumptions. ETL tools like Informatica and DataStage are relics of the on-prem era.

The Real Trade-offs

  • ETL reduces warehouse costs: Transforming before loading means less data in your warehouse and fewer compute cycles. When warehouse compute costs $400+/hour, pre-filtering matters.
  • dbt's SQL-only approach has limits: Complex transformations involving ML models, external API calls, or binary data processing don't fit SQL. You still need Python/Spark for those.
  • ELT enables agility: Raw data in the warehouse means analysts can build new models without waiting for engineering to modify ETL pipelines. Self-service analytics becomes possible.
  • Version-controlled transformations are non-negotiable: dbt brought software engineering practices (git, testing, CI/CD) to data transformations. Whatever approach you use, this practice should be standard.
  • The real answer is both: Use EL tools (Fivetran, Airbyte) for ingestion, dbt for in-warehouse transforms, and keep ETL for edge cases that need pre-processing. Purity helps nobody.
