The Real Trade-offs

  • Let's Encrypt solved the web: 300 million+ certificates issued, HTTPS adoption went from 40% to 90%+. For public websites, the certificate management problem is solved.
  • Internal services need private CAs: You can't use Let's Encrypt for internal hostnames, service mesh certificates, or non-HTTP protocols. Enterprise PKI is a different problem entirely.
  • 90-day certificates are a feature: Short-lived certs reduce exposure window. But they also mean if your renewal automation breaks, you have 90 days before outage — not 365.
  • Certificate lifecycle management is the real challenge: Enterprises need visibility into all certs, expiry alerts, compliance reporting, and rotation automation. That's a platform problem, not a CA problem.
  • EV certificates are dead: Browsers removed the green bar. EV certs offer zero user-visible trust signals. They're a $500 tax for no practical benefit.
