The Real Trade-offs

  • Overlapping CIDRs kill peering: VPC Peering and Transit Gateway both reject overlapping address ranges. If two VPCs share 10.0.0.0/16, you cannot connect them without re-IPing one — which means rebuilding everything inside it.
  • RFC 1918 space is finite: 10.0.0.0/8 gives you 16 million addresses. Sounds like a lot until you're burning /16 blocks per VPC and have 200 VPCs across 4 regions. Plan allocations centrally or suffer.
  • On-premises overlap is the real killer: Your data center already uses 10.0.0.0/16. Every VPC you create with that same range is a future Direct Connect nightmare. Map your on-prem ranges first.
  • AWS IPAM exists — use it: IPAM centralizes CIDR allocation across accounts and regions. It's free for the basic tier. There's no excuse for ad-hoc IP planning in 2026.
  • Secondary CIDRs are a band-aid: Yes, you can add secondary CIDR blocks to a VPC. But routing gets complex, some services don't support them well, and it's technical debt you'll carry forever.
