The Real Trade-offs

  • A portal is not a platform: If developers still need to file tickets to get a database or a Kubernetes namespace, you built a UI on top of your existing bottleneck. True platforms enable self-service with guardrails.
  • Catalog accuracy decays fast: Service catalogs are only useful if they're accurate. Without automated data ingestion from CI/CD, cloud APIs, and Git, your catalog is stale within weeks.
  • Platform teams need product thinking: Treat developers as customers. Do user research. Measure adoption. Iterate on feedback. Most platform teams ship features nobody asked for.
  • Not every company needs an IDP: If you have 10 developers and 3 services, a README and a Makefile are your platform. IDPs solve coordination problems that small teams don't have.
  • The best platform is invisible: Developers shouldn't have to "visit the platform." The platform should be embedded in their workflow — their IDE, their CLI, their PR checks.
