SOC 2 Type II is Table Stakes — ISO 27001 is the Real Bar

Every SaaS startup gets SOC 2 Type II because customers demand it. But SOC 2 lets you define your own controls — you're grading your own homework. ISO 27001 requires a certified management system with mandatory controls. One proves compliance, the other proves maturity.

The Real Trade-offs

  • SOC 2 scope is self-defined: You choose which Trust Services Criteria to include and define your own controls. A "SOC 2 compliant" company might have minimal security controls that technically meet their self-set bar.
  • ISO 27001 requires a management system: It's not just about controls — it requires risk assessment processes, management commitment, continuous improvement, and internal audits. It's a systematic approach to security.
  • SOC 2 is the US market standard: North American enterprise buyers demand SOC 2 reports. ISO 27001 is more recognized internationally, especially in Europe and Asia. Market reality trumps theoretical rigor.
  • ISO 27001 certification costs more: Between the ISMS implementation, surveillance audits, and recertification, ISO 27001 costs 2-3x more than SOC 2. For startups, SOC 2 provides better ROI.
  • Counter-argument — both are just baselines: Neither SOC 2 nor ISO 27001 guarantees security. They prove you have processes, not that you're secure. Real security comes from culture, not certificates.
