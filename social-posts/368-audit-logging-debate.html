The Real Trade-offs

  • Logs without analysis are just cost: CloudTrail generates GBs of events daily. Storing them in S3 costs money. If nobody queries them until a breach investigation, you're paying for retroactive forensics, not proactive security.
  • SIEM licensing is extremely expensive: Splunk, Datadog Security, and similar platforms charge per GB ingested. A mid-size AWS environment can generate $10K-50K/month in SIEM costs. The cure costs more than the disease.
  • Cloud-native alternatives reduce cost: AWS Security Hub + GuardDuty + Detective provide SIEM-like capabilities at a fraction of the cost. You don't always need a third-party SIEM.
  • Alert fatigue is worse than no alerts: A poorly tuned SIEM generates thousands of false positives. Teams start ignoring alerts, making the system less effective than having no alerts at all.
  • Counter-argument — retention alone has value: Forensic investigation after a breach requires historical logs. Even without real-time analysis, having 90 days of immutable logs in S3 is invaluable for incident response and legal compliance.
