The Real Trade-offs

  • Guardrails should be invisible: The best guardrails prevent bad things without developers noticing. SCPs that block public S3 buckets, policies that enforce tagging, cost limits that prevent runaway — all automatic.
  • Too many guardrails recreate the ticket queue: If every provisioning request needs 5 approvals, you've just replaced JIRA tickets with policy exceptions. Find the balance.
  • Cost visibility at PR time changes behavior: When developers see "this change adds $450/month" in their PR, they think about right-sizing. Infracost in CI makes cost a first-class concern.
  • Orphan resource cleanup is essential: Self-service creates resources. Self-service rarely cleans them up. Automated tagging, TTLs on dev resources, and regular audits are non-negotiable.
  • Start with a curated menu, not a blank canvas: Offer pre-approved Terraform modules or Crossplane compositions. Developers pick from a menu of secure, cost-optimized options.
