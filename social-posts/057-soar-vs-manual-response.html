Your SOAR playbook just auto-blocked the CEO's IP because they triggered a brute-force alert from a hotel WiFi. Automated remediation without context is a self-inflicted denial of service. But manual response at 3AM when the SOC is understaffed isn't an option either.

The Real Trade-offs

  • Speed matters in incident response: A ransomware attack can encrypt your entire environment in 45 minutes. Waiting for a human to wake up and approve a network isolation is a luxury you can't afford.
  • False positives cause real outages: Auto-blocking IPs, disabling accounts, or isolating hosts based on noisy detections creates more incidents than it prevents.
  • Tiered automation is the answer: Auto-enrich and auto-triage everything. Auto-remediate only high-confidence, low-blast-radius actions. Require human approval for anything that affects production.
  • SOC burnout is the real threat: Analysts processing 500+ alerts daily burn out in months. SOAR that handles L1 triage and enrichment keeps humans focused on real threats.
  • Testing playbooks is hard: Unlike code, you can't unit test a playbook that disables a production user. One wrong condition and you've locked out your entire workforce.
