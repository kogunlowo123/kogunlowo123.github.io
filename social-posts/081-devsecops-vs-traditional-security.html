The Real Trade-offs

  • Automation misses context: A scanner can find an open S3 bucket. It cannot determine whether your business logic allows unauthorized data access through a legitimate API. Human review catches design flaws that tools never will.
  • Speed vs thoroughness: DevSecOps pipelines give results in minutes. A proper security architecture review takes days. When you deploy 50 times a day, which model actually works?
  • Developer resistance kills adoption: Force developers to fix every SAST finding before merge and they'll start suppressing alerts. Traditional security reviews at least have accountability — someone signed off.
  • Compliance demands documentation: Auditors want evidence of human judgment, not just green CI checks. Regulated industries often require a named security reviewer on every change — automation alone won't satisfy SOX or PCI.
  • The real answer is layered: Automated scanning for known patterns, human review for architecture changes. But nobody wants to fund both — so you pick one and pretend it's enough.
