The Real Trade-offs

  • Identity scales to SaaS: Your workforce uses 200+ SaaS apps. Network controls are meaningless for Salesforce, Workday, and M365 — identity and conditional access are the only control plane that exists.
  • Network controls stop lateral movement: Once an identity is compromised, the attacker IS the user. Micro-segmentation limits blast radius in ways that identity policies cannot — you can't revoke an identity you don't know is compromised.
  • Machine-to-machine has no identity: Service accounts, API keys, and workload identities are the weakest link. Network isolation between microservices provides defense when service mesh mTLS isn't implemented.
  • Cloud-native favors identity: In AWS, IAM policies are more granular than security groups. You can restrict S3 access to a specific role, IP range, and VPC endpoint — that's identity AND network working together.
  • The attacker doesn't care about your model: Advanced threats exploit both layers. Building defense-in-depth means investing in both. The debate isn't which one — it's which one gets the budget first when you can't do both well.
