The Real Trade-offs

  • Production is the only real environment: Staging doesn't have your traffic patterns, data volumes, or integration dependencies. Chaos in staging proves your staging works — not your production.
  • But production chaos requires maturity: You need blast radius controls, abort conditions, observability, and runbooks before you inject failures. Most teams aren't there yet.
  • Start small, then grow: Begin with game days in staging. Graduate to chaos in production during low-traffic windows. Eventually run continuous chaos. This is a maturity journey, not a day-one decision.
  • The Netflix argument is survivorship bias: We hear about Netflix's success. We don't hear about the companies that caused outages with premature production chaos and quietly stopped.
  • Chaos without observability is just breaking things: If you can't observe the impact of your chaos experiment in real-time, you're not doing science — you're just causing incidents.
