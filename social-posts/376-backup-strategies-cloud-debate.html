The Real Trade-offs

  • Snapshots alone are not a backup strategy: EBS snapshots in the same account can be deleted by a compromised admin. Cross-account, cross-region copies with immutability are the minimum viable backup for production workloads.
  • Continuous backup (PITR) is essential for databases: RDS automated backups with 35-day retention and point-in-time recovery to any second gives you RPO measured in seconds, not hours. The 5-minute backup window gap is your only exposure.
  • Immutable backups are your ransomware insurance: AWS Backup Vault Lock, S3 Object Lock, and Azure Immutable Blob Storage prevent anyone — including root accounts — from deleting backups within the retention period.
  • Counter-argument — immutability creates cost bloat: WORM retention policies mean you cannot delete backups early, even if the data is obsolete. Over-provisioning retention periods can lead to significant storage costs that compound over years.
  • The 3-2-1-1 rule is the modern standard: 3 copies, 2 different media types, 1 offsite, 1 immutable. Cloud makes the offsite and immutable parts trivial — there's no excuse for not implementing this today.
