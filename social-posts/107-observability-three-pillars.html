The Three Pillars of Observability Are Incomplete

Logs, metrics, traces. The holy trinity of observability. Except... where are continuous profiling, real user monitoring, and change events? The three pillars model was a marketing simplification that became gospel. Real observability needs at least five signals.

The Real Trade-offs

  • Profiling finds what traces miss: A trace shows a function took 500ms. A profile shows it was due to excessive GC from a memory leak. Without profiling, you know the "where" but not the "why."
  • RUM closes the gap to user experience: Your backend metrics say p99 is 200ms. But the user's browser spent 3 seconds on DNS, TLS, and JS parsing. Server-side observability is half the picture.
  • Change events are the missing correlator: "Why did latency spike at 2pm?" Overlay deployment events, config changes, and feature flag toggles. Without this, you're guessing at causation.
  • Three pillars is a useful simplification: Counter-argument: most teams haven't mastered logs, metrics, and traces yet. Adding more signals before nailing the basics just creates more noise.
  • Cost grows with every signal: Each observability signal has ingestion, storage, and query costs. The five-signal approach is ideal but expensive. Prioritize based on your failure modes.
