OPA Gatekeeper Is Over-Engineered — Kyverno Wins

Learning Rego to write Kubernetes policies is like learning a new programming language just to say "don't run containers as root." Kyverno does the same thing in plain YAML that any K8s engineer already knows. Why make policy harder than it needs to be?

The Real Trade-offs

  • Rego is powerful but steep: OPA's Rego language can express complex policies that YAML can't. But 90% of K8s policies are simple validations that don't need a Turing-complete language.
  • Kyverno can mutate and generate: Beyond validation, Kyverno can auto-add labels, inject sidecars, and generate NetworkPolicies. OPA Gatekeeper is validation-only without custom controllers.
  • OPA is not K8s-only: OPA works for API authorization, Terraform, Envoy, and more. Kyverno is Kubernetes-specific. If you need policy across your stack, OPA is the unified choice.
  • Testing and debugging: Rego has a testing framework, playground, and profiler. Kyverno policies are harder to test in isolation because they depend on the K8s API server context.
  • Community and ecosystem: Both are CNCF projects. OPA has broader adoption outside K8s. Kyverno is growing fast specifically in the K8s space. Pick based on your scope.
