Not All Toil Should Be Eliminated

SRE orthodoxy says toil is the enemy. But spending 3 days automating a task you do once a quarter is itself wasteful. Some toil provides system understanding that automation removes. The engineer who manually deploys understands the deployment — the one who clicks a button might not.

The Real Trade-offs

  • The XKCD chart is real: If you spend 3 days automating a task that takes 5 minutes quarterly, you'll break even in 36 years. ROI matters. Not everything deserves a script.
  • Manual work builds mental models: Engineers who manually investigate alerts develop intuition about system behavior. Full automation can create operators who can't debug when the automation fails.
  • Toil identification is the first value: Even if you don't automate everything, measuring and categorizing toil reveals where your team's time goes. That visibility alone drives better prioritization.
  • Google's 50% toil budget is aspirational: Most SRE teams spend 80%+ on toil. Getting to 50% requires organizational commitment to staffing and prioritizing automation projects.
  • Automate the dangerous toil first: Manual steps in security patching, access provisioning, and incident response are both toil and risk. Automate where errors have the highest cost.
