The Real Trade-offs

  • VPN = Castle-and-moat: Once inside, lateral movement is trivial. Zero Trust treats every request as potentially hostile regardless of network location.
  • Legacy apps need VPNs: Not every application supports OIDC/SAML. Some enterprises still run apps that require network-level access — Zero Trust can't solve everything.
  • Zero Trust is complex: It requires identity governance, device compliance, micro-segmentation, and continuous verification. That's 10x the engineering effort of spinning up a VPN.
  • Cost matters: A VPN concentrator is cheap. A full Zero Trust stack with ZTNA, CASB, SWG, and identity governance can cost millions annually.
  • Hybrid is reality: Most enterprises will run both for years. The "VPN is dead" narrative ignores operational reality and migration timelines.
