Serverless Databases Aren't Actually Serverless

Serverless databases aren't actually serverless. Aurora Serverless has minimum ACUs, DynamoDB has provisioned mode, and every "serverless" database still requires you to think about capacity, connections, and scaling configuration.

The Real Trade-offs

  • DynamoDB on-demand is the closest to true serverless: Pay per request, no minimum cost (if zero traffic), scales to millions of requests. But single-table design is a learning cliff.
  • Aurora Serverless v2 always costs something: Minimum 0.5 ACU = ~$43/month even with zero queries. That's not serverless, that's "auto-scaling with a floor."
  • Connection pooling is still your problem: Lambda + RDS creates connection storms. RDS Proxy adds $20+/month per instance. DynamoDB avoids this entirely with HTTP-based access.
  • Third-party databases are innovating faster: Neon scales Postgres to zero, PlanetScale offers database branching, Fauna gives you ACID with global distribution. AWS is playing catch-up.
  • The "serverless" label is marketing: True serverless means zero cost at zero usage, instant scaling, and no capacity planning. Only DynamoDB on-demand mode and a few third-party options truly qualify.
