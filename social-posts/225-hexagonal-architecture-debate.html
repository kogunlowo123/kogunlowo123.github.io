Hexagonal Architecture Is Ports and Adapters Rebranded

Hexagonal architecture is ports and adapters rebranded. The hexagon shape is arbitrary, the pattern is the same as Clean Architecture's dependency rule, and the industry keeps renaming the same concept every 5 years. The value is real — the marketing is exhausting.

The Real Trade-offs

  • The core idea is genuinely valuable: Business logic does not know about HTTP, databases, or message queues. Adapters translate between the outside world and your domain. This enables testing and flexibility.
  • Driving vs driven ports is the key insight: Driving ports (API, CLI, events) push into your domain. Driven ports (database, email, cache) are used by your domain. This asymmetry matters more than the hexagon shape.
  • Adapter swapping rarely happens: The promise is "swap DynamoDB for PostgreSQL by changing one adapter." In practice, teams almost never swap databases. The real value is testability, not swappability.
  • Serverless naturally enforces this: Lambda handlers ARE driving adapters. DynamoDB clients ARE driven adapters. The function handler IS the port. Serverless teams do hexagonal architecture without knowing it.
  • Stop debating names, apply the principle: Hexagonal, Clean, Onion, Ports and Adapters — they all enforce dependency inversion. Pick one name, document it for your team, and move on to building features.
