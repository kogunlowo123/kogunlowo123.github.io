The Real Trade-offs

  • Paper RTOs vs real RTOs: The gap between documented and actual recovery time is typically 5-10x. A "15-minute RTO" often means 15 minutes of automated steps plus 3 hours of human decision-making and debugging.
  • RPO depends on replication lag: Your RPO of zero assumes synchronous replication. But cross-region replication is asynchronous by default, meaning you could lose minutes or hours of data.
  • Testing is expensive but necessary: A proper DR test requires coordination, downtime windows, and engineering time. But the cost of a failed real DR event is orders of magnitude higher.
  • Game days expose the real gaps: Scheduled DR tests always go better than real outages because people are prepared. Unannounced tests reveal what actually happens when the pager goes off at 3 AM.
  • Counter-argument — perfect testing is impossible: You can never fully simulate production conditions. At some point, you have to accept that DR plans are probabilistic, not deterministic.
