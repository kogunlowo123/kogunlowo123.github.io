Terraform Cloud Is Overpriced CI for Terraform

Terraform Cloud charges per resource managed. At scale, that's thousands of dollars monthly for what is essentially: run terraform plan on PR, run terraform apply on merge. Atlantis does this for free, self-hosted, with full control. Is the managed experience worth the premium?

The Real Trade-offs

  • TFC pricing scales painfully: At $0.00014/hour per resource, managing 1000 resources costs ~$100/month. At 10,000 resources, it's $1000/month. Atlantis on a small EC2 instance costs $30/month regardless of scale.
  • Atlantis maintenance is real work: You need to deploy, secure, upgrade, and monitor Atlantis. Webhook configuration, SSL, authentication, and HA all fall on your team. TFC handles all of this.
  • Sentinel is powerful but proprietary: Sentinel policies are deeply integrated with TFC but lock you in. OPA/Conftest works with any tool and has a larger community. The trade-off is integration depth vs portability.
  • TFC's value is in governance: For enterprises needing audit logs, SSO, role-based access, and approval workflows, TFC provides out-of-box compliance. Building this with Atlantis requires significant custom work.
  • Spacelift and env0 are the middle ground: These alternatives offer TFC-like features at lower prices with more flexibility. The IaC CI/CD market has matured beyond the TFC vs Atlantis binary.
