Quantum Computing on Cloud Is a Decade from Useful

AWS Braket, Azure Quantum, and IBM Quantum are available today. But what real-world problem can they solve that a GPU cluster can't? Error rates are too high, qubit counts too low, and the algorithms for business problems don't exist yet. Quantum cloud is a science experiment, not a business tool.

The Real Trade-offs

  • Error correction is unsolved: Current quantum computers make too many errors for useful computation. Fault-tolerant quantum computing requires millions of physical qubits — we have hundreds.
  • Cryptography is the real threat: Post-quantum cryptography migration should start now. When quantum computers break RSA, you need to have already migrated. Start planning today.
  • Quantum simulation has promise: Drug discovery, materials science, and molecular simulation — these are genuine use cases where quantum advantage may arrive first.
  • Cloud access democratizes research: Even if quantum isn't production-ready, cloud quantum services let teams experiment without buying $15M hardware. That has value.
  • Quantum-inspired algorithms work now: Classical algorithms inspired by quantum approaches (quantum annealing simulations) run on regular hardware and solve optimization problems today.
