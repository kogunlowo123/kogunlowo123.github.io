The Real Trade-offs

  • Individual metrics are toxic: The moment you rank engineers by commits or PRs, you incentivize small meaningless PRs and discourage deep architectural work that takes weeks.
  • DORA metrics work at team level: Deployment frequency and lead time are useful team-level signals. They become harmful when used to compare or rank individual engineers.
  • Surveys reveal what metrics hide: Developer satisfaction surveys catch problems (tooling friction, unclear requirements) that no automated metric will ever surface.
  • Measurement without action is pointless: Dashboards that nobody acts on are waste. If you measure lead time but never invest in CI/CD improvements, why bother?
  • The best proxy is outcomes: Measure what the team delivers to customers, not how many PRs each person wrote. Impact over activity, always.
