FedRAMP is a Barrier to Innovation, Not Security

FedRAMP authorization costs $2-5M and takes 12-18 months. It has 325+ controls for Moderate impact level. The result? Federal agencies are stuck using technology that was cutting-edge when the authorization process started. FedRAMP protects incumbents, not data.

The Real Trade-offs

  • Authorization timelines stifle innovation: By the time a SaaS product gets FedRAMP authorized, the commercial version is 2 years ahead in features. Federal agencies get yesterday's technology at premium prices.
  • Cost creates a barrier to entry: $2-5M for authorization means only well-funded companies can serve the federal market. Startups with superior technology are locked out, reducing competition.
  • Continuous monitoring is the real value: FedRAMP's continuous monitoring requirements (monthly vulnerability scans, annual assessments) provide ongoing security assurance that point-in-time certifications don't.
  • The "do once, use many" promise is real: Once authorized, agencies can reuse the authorization without duplicate assessments. This saves individual agencies significant time and money.
  • Counter-argument — federal data needs higher protection: Government systems are high-value targets for nation-state actors. The rigorous FedRAMP process ensures cloud providers meet a baseline that commercial certifications don't require.
