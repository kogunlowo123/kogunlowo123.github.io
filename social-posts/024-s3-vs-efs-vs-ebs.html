EFS Is the Worst Storage Service on AWS

EFS costs $0.30/GB/month — 10x more than S3 Standard and 3x more than gp3 EBS. Its latency is unpredictable, throughput bursting is confusing, and most teams using it should just use S3 with FUSE or EBS with multi-attach. EFS is the storage equivalent of paying premium for mediocre.

The Real Trade-offs

  • S3 is 13x cheaper than EFS and handles 5,500 GET requests/sec per prefix
  • EBS gp3 gives 3,000 IOPS baseline for $0.08/GB — predictable and fast
  • EFS is the only option for shared file systems across multiple EC2/ECS/Lambda — that's its real use case
  • EFS Infrequent Access tier at $0.025/GB makes it competitive if most data is cold
  • FSx for Lustre is better than EFS for high-performance shared file system needs
