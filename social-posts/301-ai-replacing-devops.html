The Real Trade-offs

  • Repetitive tasks are first to go: Alert triage, log parsing, basic runbook execution — AI handles these today with 90%+ accuracy. That's a large chunk of junior DevOps work.
  • Architecture decisions still need humans: AI can generate Terraform, but deciding between EKS and ECS based on team capability and business context requires human judgment.
  • AI augments, not replaces (for now): The real shift is 1 engineer + AI doing the work of 3. Teams shrink, but roles elevate to platform architecture and strategy.
  • Risk of over-automation: Auto-remediation without guardrails caused major outages at multiple companies. The human-in-the-loop isn't optional for critical systems.
  • Upskilling is non-negotiable: Engineers who learn to leverage AI tools multiply their output 5x. Those who ignore AI become the 50% that gets replaced.
