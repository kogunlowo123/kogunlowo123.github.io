Choreography Without Tracing Is Chaos

Choreography without tracing is chaos. Services reacting to events independently sounds elegant — until an order gets stuck and nobody knows which of 12 services failed to process it. Without distributed tracing, choreography is organized anarchy.

The Real Trade-offs

  • Choreography scales team autonomy: Each team owns their service and reacts to events independently. No central orchestrator to bottleneck development. But this freedom creates invisible coupling through event schemas.
  • Orchestration gives you a visual workflow: Step Functions shows exactly where an order is in its lifecycle. Choreography? You need to query 12 services to piece together the current state of a single order.
  • Correlation IDs are non-negotiable: Every event must carry a correlation ID that flows through the entire chain. Without it, connecting a failed payment notification to its originating order is impossible.
  • Hybrid is the pragmatic answer: Use orchestration for critical business flows (order processing, payment) and choreography for non-critical reactions (analytics, notifications, audit logging).
  • Event schema evolution breaks choreography: When Service A changes its event schema, every consumer must adapt. Without a schema registry enforcing backward compatibility, deployments become coordinated releases.
