AWS Direct Connect vs VPN: When Dedicated Circuits Beat Encrypted Tunnels

Direct Connect gives you a private 1 Gbps or 10 Gbps pipe straight into AWS with sub-5ms latency. Site-to-Site VPN costs 90% less and deploys in minutes. But the real question is: does your workload actually need dedicated bandwidth, or are you just paying for peace of mind?

The Real Trade-offs

  • Cost gap is enormous: A 1 Gbps Direct Connect port costs ~$220/month plus cross-connect fees and partner charges — easily $1,000+ monthly. A Site-to-Site VPN connection is $36/month. For workloads under 500 Mbps sustained, the VPN math almost always wins.
  • Latency consistency matters more than speed: Direct Connect doesn't make packets faster — it makes latency predictable. VPN latency fluctuates with internet congestion. If your app tolerates 20-50ms jitter, VPN is fine. Real-time trading or database replication? You need DX.
  • Data transfer costs differ significantly: DX data transfer out is $0.02/GB versus $0.09/GB over the internet (VPN). At 10 TB/month egress, DX saves $700/month on transfer alone, which offsets the port cost.
  • Deployment time is the hidden factor: VPN deploys in 30 minutes. Direct Connect takes 2-12 weeks for cross-connect provisioning. In disaster recovery scenarios, VPN as a backup is non-negotiable even if DX is your primary link.
  • The best answer is usually both: Most enterprises run DX as primary with VPN failover. AWS Transit Gateway makes this pattern straightforward. Pure VPN shops tend to be startups or low-throughput workloads; pure DX shops are paying for simplicity they might not need.
