The Real Trade-offs

  • Incident response needs instant access: When production is down at 3AM, waiting 30 minutes for PIM approval and MFA verification costs real money. Break-glass procedures must be fast.
  • PIM friction reduces risky changes: Having to justify and activate elevated access makes engineers think twice before running destructive commands. Friction is a feature, not a bug.
  • Audit trails are invaluable: PIM logs every activation — who, when, why, and for how long. Standing access has no audit trail because there's nothing to log.
  • Not everything supports JIT: AWS root account, hypervisor access, and legacy systems often require standing credentials. The goal is minimizing standing access, not eliminating it entirely.
  • Access reviews catch creep: Monthly access reviews through Entra ID Governance ensure that eligible roles are still justified. Without reviews, eligible access becomes de facto standing access.
