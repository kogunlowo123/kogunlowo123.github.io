The Real Trade-offs

  • Pilot Light costs 5-15% of production: You only keep data replication and minimal core services running. Compute spins up on-demand during failover — perfect for workloads with 1-4 hour RTO.
  • Warm Standby costs 30-50% of production: A scaled-down but fully functional copy runs 24/7. You're paying for idle compute "just in case." Most teams never actually fail over.
  • The automation gap is the real risk: Pilot Light works only if your IaC can reliably spin up infrastructure in minutes. Without tested runbooks, your "30-minute failover" becomes a 6-hour scramble.
  • Database promotion is the bottleneck: Both strategies share the same pain point — promoting a read replica to primary. This step alone can take 10-30 minutes and is the most failure-prone part of DR.
  • Counter-argument — Warm Standby enables testing: Having a running environment means you can continuously validate DR readiness. Pilot Light only proves it works when you actually need it.
