The Real Trade-offs

  • SSO tax is real: Many SaaS vendors charge 2-5x more for SSO integration. That pricing model actively discourages good security practices and punishes smaller companies.
  • Single point of failure: If your IdP goes down, everyone is locked out of everything. Entra ID outages have taken down entire enterprises. Local accounts are a resilience strategy.
  • Break-glass accounts need local auth: You always need at least one local admin account for disaster recovery. SSO can't be your only access path.
  • Not everything supports SAML/OIDC: Legacy apps, network devices, IoT systems, and some CLI tools don't support federated auth. Local accounts are sometimes the only option.
  • Offboarding is the killer use case: Disabling one SSO account revokes access everywhere instantly. With local accounts, you're playing whack-a-mole across dozens of services.
