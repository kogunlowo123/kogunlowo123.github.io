The Real Trade-offs

  • Ambiguity is the enemy: "Highly available" means different things to different people. NL interfaces must resolve ambiguity or produce dangerous defaults.
  • Democratization vs. danger: Non-engineers provisioning cloud resources sounds great until someone deploys a public S3 bucket because they didn't know to say "private."
  • HCL becomes intermediate representation: Just as nobody writes assembly anymore, HCL might become the IR that AI generates and humans occasionally review.
  • Guardrails are essential: NL-to-Terraform only works safely with policy engines (OPA, Sentinel) that reject insecure or non-compliant generated code automatically.
  • The skill shift is real: Future cloud engineers won't write HCL — they'll design policy constraints, review AI output, and architect at a higher level of abstraction.
