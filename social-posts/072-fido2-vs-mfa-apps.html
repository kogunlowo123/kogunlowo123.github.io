FIDO2 Hardware Keys Should Be Mandatory for All Engineers

Google mandated YubiKeys for 85,000 employees and phishing attacks dropped to zero. TOTP apps can be phished with real-time proxy attacks. SMS codes are trivially intercepted. Why are we still treating hardware keys as optional?

The Real Trade-offs

  • Cost and logistics: YubiKeys cost $50-70 each. For 1,000 engineers with backup keys, that's $100K-140K. Plus shipping, replacement, and inventory management. TOTP apps are free.
  • Passkeys are the middle ground: Platform authenticators (Face ID, Windows Hello) offer phishing-resistant auth without hardware keys. They're free, built-in, and increasingly supported.
  • Lost key recovery is painful: Engineer loses their YubiKey on Friday night. They can't access anything until Monday when IT ships a replacement. Recovery workflows must be bulletproof.
  • Not everything supports FIDO2: Legacy apps, VPNs, SSH, and some SaaS platforms still only support TOTP or password auth. You can't go FIDO2-only until your entire stack supports it.
  • Phishing resistance is non-negotiable for high-value targets: Engineers with production access, admin credentials, and code signing keys should absolutely require hardware keys. The risk justifies the cost.
