The Real Trade-offs

  • Falco detects, it doesn't respond: Falco tells you something bad happened. Sysdig can automatically kill the container. Detection without response is just logging with extra steps.
  • Rule writing is an art: Falco rules require deep Linux/syscall knowledge. Sysdig provides curated, maintained rulesets with threat intelligence. Your team probably can't write better rules.
  • Scale changes everything: Falco on 10 nodes is easy. Falco on 10,000 nodes needs centralized management, rule distribution, and alert aggregation — all things Sysdig provides out of the box.
  • Compliance needs reporting: Auditors want dashboards, reports, and evidence trails. Falco gives you stdout logs. Sysdig gives you SOC 2 and PCI-DSS reports.
  • CNCF backing matters: As a graduated CNCF project, Falco has community-driven development, vendor neutrality, and long-term sustainability that commercial tools can't guarantee.
