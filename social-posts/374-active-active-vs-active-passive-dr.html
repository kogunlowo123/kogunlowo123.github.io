The Real Trade-offs

  • Active-Active doubles your operational complexity: You need conflict resolution for concurrent writes, bi-directional replication monitoring, and region-aware application logic. Most teams underestimate this by 3-5x.
  • Active-Passive failover can be under 60 seconds: With Route 53 health checks (10-second intervals), pre-warmed compute, and automated RDS promotion, you can achieve RTO well under a minute at a fraction of Active-Active cost.
  • Data consistency is the hidden nightmare: Active-Active with eventual consistency means your application must handle stale reads, write conflicts, and split-brain scenarios. DynamoDB Global Tables handle this, but relational databases do not.
  • Counter-argument — Active-Active improves latency: Users get routed to the nearest region, cutting latency by 50-200ms. For global SaaS products, this latency improvement justifies the complexity.
  • The middle ground is read-active, write-passive: Serve reads from both regions but funnel all writes to a single primary. This gives you latency benefits without write-conflict complexity.
