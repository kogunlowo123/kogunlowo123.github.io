The Real Trade-offs

  • Copilot excels at boilerplate: Standard VPC modules, IAM policies, and S3 bucket configurations are generated with proper structure, tags, and encryption defaults. It's genuinely faster.
  • CodeWhisperer knows AWS better: For AWS-specific resources, CodeWhisperer's suggestions often include nuances like proper IAM trust policies and service-linked roles that Copilot misses.
  • Neither understands your architecture: AI generates syntactically correct Terraform but can't reason about your blast radius, dependency chains, or state management strategy.
  • Security blind spots persist: AI-generated code frequently passes validation but includes overly permissive IAM policies or public-facing resources without proper guardrails.
  • The real skill is reviewing AI output: The future DevOps engineer is an AI output reviewer. Knowing what's wrong with generated code is harder than writing it from scratch.
