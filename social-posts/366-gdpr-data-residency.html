GDPR Data Residency Requirements Drive Cloud Region Selection

GDPR doesn't mandate EU-only data storage. But after Schrems II invalidated Privacy Shield, transferring personal data to US cloud regions requires SCCs, DPIAs, and supplementary measures. Most companies just keep data in eu-west-1 because it's simpler than the legal gymnastics.

The Real Trade-offs

  • Data residency is simpler than transfer mechanisms: Keeping EU personal data in EU regions avoids the complexity of Standard Contractual Clauses, Data Protection Impact Assessments, and supplementary measures.
  • Multi-region adds operational complexity: Separate data stores per region means separate backup strategies, separate DR plans, and complex data routing logic. Your architecture becomes geo-aware by necessity.
  • Cloud providers offer residency guarantees: AWS, Azure, and GCP now offer data residency commitments and sovereign cloud options. The tooling exists — the complexity is in the application layer.
  • Data residency doesn't equal data protection: Keeping data in the EU doesn't protect it from breaches, insider threats, or government access requests. Residency is a legal requirement, not a security measure.
  • Counter-argument — the regulatory landscape keeps shifting: The EU-US Data Privacy Framework may be challenged again. Building architecture around current regulations risks expensive refactoring when rules change.
