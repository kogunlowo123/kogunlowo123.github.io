The Real Trade-offs

  • 2 minutes is not enough for most apps: Your app needs to checkpoint state, drain connections, deregister from the load balancer, and gracefully shut down — all in 120 seconds. Most monoliths can't do this.
  • Diversification is mandatory: Using a single instance type is a recipe for mass interruption. You need 10+ instance types across 3+ AZs. The capacity-optimized allocation strategy helps but doesn't eliminate risk.
  • Kubernetes makes it easier — with Karpenter: Karpenter automatically diversifies instance types, handles graceful draining via pod disruption budgets, and falls back to on-demand. It's the best Spot management tool available.
  • Stateless workloads are the sweet spot: Web servers, CI/CD runners, batch processing, and data pipelines handle interruptions naturally. Databases, message brokers, and stateful services should never run on Spot.
  • The savings math changes with interruption frequency: If you're interrupted frequently and fall back to on-demand, your effective discount shrinks. Track your actual blended rate, not the Spot price alone.
