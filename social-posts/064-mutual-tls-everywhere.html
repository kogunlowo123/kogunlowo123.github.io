The Real Trade-offs

  • Network trust is not enough: If an attacker compromises one pod, they can sniff unencrypted traffic between services. mTLS prevents eavesdropping and ensures identity verification at every hop.
  • Service meshes add operational complexity: Sidecar proxies consume CPU/memory, add latency, and create new failure modes. For small teams, the ops burden outweighs the security benefit.
  • Compliance often requires encryption in transit: PCI-DSS, HIPAA, and SOC 2 all require encryption of sensitive data in transit — even inside your network. mTLS is the simplest way to prove compliance.
  • Linkerd makes it easy: Unlike Istio's complexity, Linkerd adds mTLS automatically with zero config. The "mTLS is too complex" argument is becoming outdated as tooling improves.
  • Application-level auth might be enough: If every service validates JWTs and uses OAuth2, you already have identity verification. mTLS adds transport-level security but may be redundant.
