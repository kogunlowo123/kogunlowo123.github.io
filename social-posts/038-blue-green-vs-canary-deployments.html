Blue-Green vs Canary Deployments: Which Strategy Saves You From Outages?

Blue-green deployments give you instant rollback — but you're paying for double the infrastructure during every release. Canary deployments expose only 5% of users to risk — but if your monitoring isn't rock solid, you won't catch the failure in time. Pick your risk profile.

The Real Trade-offs

  • Blue-green with Azure Deployment Slots gives you zero-downtime swaps — but database migrations can't be swapped back. Schema changes break the illusion of instant rollback.
  • Canary with CodeDeploy lets you shift 10% → 50% → 100% of traffic gradually. But you need CloudWatch anomaly detection to catch regressions automatically.
  • Kubernetes rolling updates are neither blue-green nor canary — they're a hybrid that replaces pods gradually but offers no traffic shaping.
  • Blue-green doubles your compute cost during deployment. For a 100-node cluster, that's potentially thousands of dollars per release window.
  • Canary deployments require observability maturity — GCP Cloud Deploy integrates verification steps, but your team needs SLO-based alerting to make it work.
  • The hidden winner: progressive delivery with feature flags. Ship the code to everyone, toggle the feature for 5%. No extra infrastructure, no traffic splitting — just AWS AppConfig feature flags.
