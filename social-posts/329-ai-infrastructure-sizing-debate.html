AI Infrastructure Sizing: GPU Clusters Are the New Money Pit

A single p5.48xlarge instance with 8x H100 GPUs costs $98/hour on AWS. Leave a training cluster running over a weekend and you have burned $4,700. Over-provision for peak training demand and GPUs sit idle 70% of the time. Under-provision and training jobs queue for days. AI infrastructure sizing is the hardest capacity planning problem in cloud computing.

The Real Trade-offs

  • Separate training from inference infrastructure: Training is bursty and benefits from spot instances. Inference is steady-state and needs reserved capacity. Mixing them on the same cluster wastes money.
  • Spot GPUs save 60-70% but need checkpointing: Use spot for training with frequent model checkpointing. When instances are reclaimed, resume from the last checkpoint. This is non-negotiable for cost-effective training.
  • Right-size GPU memory, not just count: Running a 7B parameter model on an A100 80GB is wasteful — an L4 with 24GB handles it fine at a fraction of the cost. Match GPU memory to model size.
  • Inference needs different GPUs than training: Training wants H100s for raw throughput. Inference wants Inferentia2 or T4 GPUs optimized for low-latency serving. Using training GPUs for inference is like hauling groceries in a semi truck.
  • Start with managed services, then optimize: Use SageMaker or Vertex AI endpoints first. Only move to self-managed GPU clusters when you have proven that managed pricing exceeds self-managed costs by at least 40%.
