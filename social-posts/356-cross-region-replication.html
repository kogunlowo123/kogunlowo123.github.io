Cross-Region Replication Costs More Than Most DR Plans

Cross-region replication for a 10TB database costs $200-500/month in data transfer alone — before storage and compute. For many workloads, periodic cross-region backups achieve nearly the same RPO at 10% of the cost. Are you paying for DR or paying for peace of mind?

The Real Trade-offs

  • Data transfer is the hidden cost: AWS charges $0.02/GB for inter-region data transfer. A database with 100GB of daily change data costs $60/month just for replication bandwidth — multiply by every replicated service.
  • Continuous replication gives near-zero RPO: If you truly need RPO of seconds, cross-region replication is the only option. But most workloads can tolerate RPO of 1-4 hours, making periodic backups sufficient.
  • Storage duplication doubles costs: Running a full copy of your database in another region means paying for compute, storage, and IOPS in both regions. That's 2x your database bill for a capability you hope to never use.
  • Backup-based DR is simpler to manage: Cross-region replicas need monitoring, version compatibility tracking, and failover testing. Backup restore is a simpler operation with fewer things that can go wrong.
  • Counter-argument — downtime cost exceeds replication cost: If one hour of downtime costs $100K+, the $500/month for cross-region replication is a rounding error. The math depends entirely on your business impact analysis.
