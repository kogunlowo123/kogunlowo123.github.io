The Real Trade-offs

  • Backup completion != backup validity: A successful backup job means bytes were written to storage. It doesn't mean those bytes can reconstruct your database, application state, or configuration.
  • Automated restore testing is cheap insurance: Spinning up a test environment weekly to validate restores costs a few dollars. Discovering a corrupt backup during an outage costs millions.
  • Restore time is part of your RTO: Even perfect backups take time to restore. A 2TB database restore from S3 can take hours. If your RTO is 30 minutes, backup-based recovery won't save you.
  • Configuration drift breaks restores: Your backup contains last week's data, but your application has changed since then. Schema migrations, config changes, and dependency updates can make old backups unusable.
  • Counter-argument — testing everything is impractical: Full restore tests for every service every week is operationally expensive. Risk-based prioritization means you test critical systems and trust the rest.
