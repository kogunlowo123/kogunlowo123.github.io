PrivateLink Should Replace VPC Peering Everywhere

VPC Peering opens your entire network to another VPC. PrivateLink exposes a single service endpoint. One is a network-level trust relationship. The other is a service-level contract. Why are we still choosing the riskier option?

The Real Trade-offs

  • PrivateLink is zero-trust by design: Only the specific service is exposed — no network-level access. The consumer VPC cannot scan, discover, or reach any other resource in the provider VPC. This is the networking equivalent of least-privilege.
  • No CIDR overlap problems: PrivateLink works even when VPCs have overlapping CIDR blocks. VPC Peering requires non-overlapping CIDRs. This alone makes PrivateLink essential for acquisitions and multi-tenant platforms.
  • Cost is higher per connection: Interface endpoints cost $0.01/hr per AZ (~$7.20/month per AZ). For dozens of services, this adds up. VPC Peering has no hourly charge — only standard data transfer.
  • Not every pattern fits: PrivateLink is great for request/response APIs. It's less ideal for bidirectional communication, database replication, or high-bandwidth streaming where peering's full network access is needed.
  • Scalability favors PrivateLink: VPC Peering has a limit of 125 connections per VPC. PrivateLink endpoints can be shared across thousands of consumer VPCs. For platform teams, PrivateLink scales better.
