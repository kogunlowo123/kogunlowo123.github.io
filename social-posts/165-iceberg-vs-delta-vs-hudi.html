The Real Trade-offs

  • Iceberg's vendor neutrality wins: No single company controls Iceberg's roadmap. Apple, Netflix, and LinkedIn are major contributors. This independence builds trust that Delta (Databricks) and Hudi (originally Uber) lack.
  • Delta has the best Spark integration: If you're a Databricks shop, Delta Lake's integration is seamless — optimized compaction, auto-tuning, and Unity Catalog. Iceberg on Databricks works but isn't the default.
  • Hudi excels at incremental processing: For use cases requiring record-level upserts and incremental queries (CDC pipelines), Hudi's copy-on-write and merge-on-read tables are purpose-built.
  • Catalog interoperability is the real battle: Iceberg's REST catalog specification enables multi-engine access. The catalog — not the table format — determines how discoverable and queryable your data is.
  • Convergence is happening: Databricks added UniForm for Iceberg compatibility. The formats are converging. In 2-3 years, the format war may be irrelevant as engines abstract it away.
