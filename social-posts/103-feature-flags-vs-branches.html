The Real Trade-offs

  • Flag sprawl is real: Without a strict expiration policy, flags accumulate. Knight Capital lost $440M partly due to a stale feature flag. Lifecycle management is non-negotiable.
  • Testing combinatorial explosion: Every flag doubles your test matrix. 10 flags = 1024 possible states. Most teams test maybe 3 of those combinations.
  • Flags enable trunk-based dev: The biggest win is decoupling deployment from release. Ship code daily, activate features when business decides. This power is worth the complexity.
  • Branches are simpler but slower: Feature branches are conceptually simpler — no runtime toggles, no flag cleanup. But they create merge debt instead of flag debt.
  • The answer is discipline: Feature flags with TTLs, automated cleanup, and flag audits are powerful. Without those guardrails, you're just trading one kind of debt for another.
