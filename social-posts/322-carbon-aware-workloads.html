The Real Trade-offs

  • Batch jobs are perfect candidates: ML training, ETL pipelines, and nightly builds can shift by hours with zero user impact. Carbon savings of 30-50% are achievable.
  • Real-time workloads can't wait: You can't delay an API response because the grid is dirty right now. Carbon-aware scheduling only works for deferrable workloads.
  • Region shifting adds latency: Moving a batch job from Virginia to Oregon for cleaner power adds data transfer costs and latency. The math doesn't always work out.
  • Data availability is improving: WattTime and Electricity Maps provide real-time carbon intensity data for most grids worldwide. The tooling is maturing rapidly.
  • Small savings scale massively: 10% carbon reduction per workload across millions of cloud instances globally amounts to significant environmental impact.
