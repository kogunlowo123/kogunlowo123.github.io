The Real Trade-offs

  • OTel auto-instrumentation gaps: Proprietary agents often have better out-of-box coverage for specific frameworks. OTel's auto-instrumentation is improving but still lags for some languages and libraries.
  • Vendor features beyond telemetry: Datadog's APM, error tracking, and anomaly detection are deeply integrated with their agent. OTel gives you raw data — you build the intelligence yourself.
  • Collector adds operational overhead: Running OTel Collectors is another thing to deploy, scale, and monitor. Proprietary agents are fire-and-forget. That simplicity has value.
  • Vendor portability is real freedom: Unhappy with your observability vendor's pricing? With OTel, swap backends in a config change. With proprietary agents, it's a re-instrumentation project.
  • The industry is converging on OTel: Even Datadog and New Relic accept OTLP. OTel is the second-most active CNCF project after Kubernetes. The momentum is unstoppable.
