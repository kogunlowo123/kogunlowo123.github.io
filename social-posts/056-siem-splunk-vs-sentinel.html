The Real Trade-offs

  • Splunk's query language is unmatched: SPL is incredibly powerful for threat hunting. KQL in Sentinel is good but lacks the depth and community content that 20 years of Splunk has built.
  • Sentinel is Azure-native: Free ingestion for Microsoft 365, Defender, and Entra logs makes it unbeatable for Microsoft shops. But non-Microsoft data ingestion costs add up fast.
  • Per-GB pricing creates perverse incentives: Teams filter out "noisy" logs to save costs. Those same logs often contain the IOCs that matter during incident response.
  • Splunk has the ecosystem: Thousands of apps, dashboards, and threat intelligence integrations on Splunkbase. Sentinel's content hub is growing but still catching up.
  • Open-source alternatives are emerging: Elastic SIEM, Wazuh, and OpenSearch are viable for teams willing to self-manage. The real debate might be "why pay for SIEM at all?"
