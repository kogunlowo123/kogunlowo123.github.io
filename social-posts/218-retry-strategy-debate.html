Exponential Backoff Without Jitter Is a DDoS on Yourself

Exponential backoff without jitter is a DDoS on yourself. When 1,000 clients fail at the same time, they all retry at 1s, 2s, 4s, 8s — in perfect synchronization. Add jitter or watch your recovering service immediately crash again.

The Real Trade-offs

  • Thundering herd kills recovery: Without jitter, all failed clients retry at the exact same intervals. The downstream service starts recovering, gets slammed by synchronized retries, and crashes again. Rinse and repeat.
  • Full jitter beats equal jitter: AWS's research shows full jitter (sleep = random(0, base * 2^attempt)) spreads load better than equal jitter (sleep = base * 2^attempt / 2 + random(0, base * 2^attempt / 2)).
  • AWS SDKs do this correctly by default: The AWS SDK includes exponential backoff with jitter. But custom HTTP clients and third-party API calls? You must implement it yourself.
  • Set a maximum retry count and cap: Unbounded retries with exponential backoff can wait hours between attempts. Cap at 3-5 retries with a maximum delay of 30-60 seconds, then fail to a DLQ.
  • Not all errors are retryable: 4xx client errors should never be retried — they will fail every time. Only retry on 5xx, timeouts, and connection errors. Retrying validation errors wastes resources.
