FinOps Teams Report to Engineering, Not Finance

Finance teams create dashboards. Engineering teams resize instances. When FinOps reports to finance, you get beautiful reports and zero action. When it reports to engineering, you get ugly spreadsheets and 30% cost reduction. Choose wisely.

The Real Trade-offs

  • Engineering owns the levers: Right-sizing, spot adoption, architecture changes, autoscaling — every meaningful cost reduction requires engineering work. Finance can identify waste; only engineering can fix it.
  • Finance brings accountability: Without financial rigor, engineering optimizes for performance, not cost. Finance-led FinOps ensures budgets exist, forecasts are accurate, and commitments are managed properly.
  • The best model is a cross-functional team: FinOps Foundation recommends a team that bridges engineering, finance, and product. Reporting line matters less than executive sponsorship and embedded engineering capacity.
  • Cultural change is the real challenge: Whether under engineering or finance, FinOps fails if engineers don't see cost as their responsibility. Unit economics in dashboards, cost in PR reviews — culture eats org charts.
  • Tooling doesn't solve people problems: CloudHealth, Kubecost, and Spot.io give you data. But data without someone empowered to act on it is just noise. The reporting line determines who has that power.
