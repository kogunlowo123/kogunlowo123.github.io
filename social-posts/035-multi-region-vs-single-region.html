Multi-Region vs Single-Region: Is Global Redundancy Worth 3x the Cost?

Everyone says "go multi-region for HA." But AWS itself runs most services in a single region with multi-AZ. If three availability zones give you 99.99% uptime, why are you paying for cross-region replication you may never need?

The Real Trade-offs

  • Multi-region with Aurora Global Database adds ~$500/month minimum just for the secondary region — before compute, NAT, and data transfer costs.
  • Single-region multi-AZ using ALB cross-zone balancing handles most failure scenarios at a fraction of the cost.
  • Cosmos DB multi-region writes solve the consistency problem but introduce conflict resolution complexity most teams underestimate.
  • Cloud Spanner is the only truly globally consistent database — but at $0.90/node/hour, you're paying enterprise prices for a feature you might not need.
  • Data sovereignty laws (GDPR, data residency) might force multi-region regardless of your HA requirements — check AWS region compliance first.
  • The middle ground: single-region active, with pilot-light DR in a second region. You get recovery without paying for full active-active.
