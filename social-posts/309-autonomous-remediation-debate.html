The Real Trade-offs

  • Speed vs. safety is the core tension: Auto-remediation reduces MTTR to seconds but introduces the risk of automated actions making incidents worse with no human oversight.
  • Blast radius matters: Restarting a single pod? Fine to automate. Scaling down a database cluster? That needs human eyes on blast radius assessment.
  • Tiered automation is the answer: Low-risk actions (restart pod, clear cache) auto-remediate. Medium-risk (scale cluster) notify and suggest. High-risk (failover database) require approval.
  • Audit trails are non-negotiable: Every automated action must be logged with full context — what triggered it, what was the system state, what changed.
  • Feedback loops prevent runaway automation: If the same remediation fires 3 times in 10 minutes, stop and escalate. Automated retry loops have caused some of the worst outages in history.
