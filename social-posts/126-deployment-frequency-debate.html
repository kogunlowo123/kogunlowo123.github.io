Congrats, you deploy 50 times a day. You also have a 30% rollback rate, no tests, and your MTTR is 4 hours. Deployment frequency without stability metrics is a vanity metric. Speed without quality isn't DevOps — it's just moving fast and breaking things.

The Real Trade-offs

  • All four DORA metrics matter together: High deployment frequency with high change failure rate means you're just creating incidents faster. Elite teams score high on all four metrics simultaneously.
  • Frequency enables stability: Counter-argument: smaller, more frequent deploys have lower blast radius and are easier to debug. Frequency and stability aren't opposed — they're correlated.
  • The metric trap: Teams optimize for the metric, not the outcome. Splitting one deployment into 5 smaller ones "improves" frequency without delivering more value.
  • Context matters: A medical device company deploying weekly with zero failures is more impressive than a startup deploying hourly with 10% rollbacks. Compare yourself to your domain, not Twitter.
  • Focus on lead time instead: Lead time (commit to production) captures both speed and process quality. A 1-hour lead time implies good CI/CD, fast tests, and streamlined reviews.
