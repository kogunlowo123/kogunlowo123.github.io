The Real Trade-offs

  • Certs are gatekeepers, not validators: Many job postings require certifications as minimum qualifications. They filter candidates, but they don't predict job performance. The cert gets you the interview; experience gets you the job.
  • Study time has opportunity cost: 100 hours studying for a cert could be spent building a real project, contributing to open source, or gaining hands-on experience. Which has more career value?
  • Certs signal dedication and baseline knowledge: Passing SA Professional requires understanding dozens of AWS services. Even if it's theoretical, it proves you invested significant time in learning the platform.
  • Consulting and partnerships require certs: AWS Partner Network tiers require certified staff. Consulting firms need certified engineers to maintain partnerships. In this context, certs have direct business value.
  • Counter-argument — certs provide structured learning: Self-directed learning often has gaps. Certification curricula ensure you learn services and patterns you'd never encounter in your daily work.
