Flexera, Gartner, and every FinOps survey says the same thing: 30% of cloud spend is wasted. That's $60 billion globally — per year. Yet most organizations treat cloud cost optimization as a "nice to have." Why?

The Real Trade-offs

  • Speed beats efficiency in high-growth: Startups and fast-growing companies rationally choose velocity over optimization. Engineering time spent optimizing costs is time not spent building features. The ROI of optimization vs. growth is the real debate.
  • Nobody gets promoted for saving money: Engineers get rewarded for shipping features and improving reliability. Cost reduction is invisible work. Until organizations celebrate savings like they celebrate launches, waste will persist.
  • The tooling gap is closing: AWS Trusted Advisor, Cost Explorer, Compute Optimizer, and third-party tools like Spot.io and CloudHealth make waste detection easier. But detection without action is just awareness.
  • Cloud providers benefit from waste: AWS, Azure, and GCP make money from your oversized instances. Their optimization tools are helpful but will never be as aggressive as third-party tools. Misaligned incentives are real.
  • The fix is cultural, not technical: Automated cleanup, FinOps practices, and cost-aware architecture reviews address symptoms. The root cause is that most organizations don't treat cloud spend as an engineering metric.
