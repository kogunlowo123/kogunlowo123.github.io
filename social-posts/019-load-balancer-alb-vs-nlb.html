ALB vs NLB — Most Teams Pick Wrong

Every team defaults to ALB because "it's the standard." But ALB adds latency with HTTP parsing, costs more with LCU-based pricing, and can't handle gRPC properly. NLB passes through TCP connections at wire speed with static IPs. You're paying for Layer 7 features you don't use.

The Real Trade-offs

  • ALB is required for path-based routing, host-based routing, and WebSocket support
  • NLB provides static IPs, preserves source IP, and handles millions of connections per second
  • ALB LCU pricing can surprise you — high connection counts and complex rules increase cost significantly
  • NLB + PrivateLink enables cross-account service exposure — ALB can't do this
  • For gRPC workloads, NLB with HTTP/2 passthrough outperforms ALB's gRPC support
