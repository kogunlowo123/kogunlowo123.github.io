Bug Bounties Are Cheaper Than Annual Pentests

You pay $150K for a 2-week pentest that finds 15 issues, 10 of which are "missing security headers." Meanwhile, a $50K bug bounty program runs 365 days a year with thousands of researchers testing your attack surface continuously. The math is obvious.

The Real Trade-offs

  • Pentests provide structured coverage: A professional pentest follows a methodology (OWASP, PTES) that systematically covers your attack surface. Bug bounties find what's interesting to researchers, not what's comprehensive.
  • Compliance requires pentests: PCI-DSS, SOC 2, and ISO 27001 require documented penetration testing. "We have a bug bounty" doesn't satisfy auditors (yet).
  • Bug bounties find the hard stuff: Researchers spend weeks chaining vulnerabilities for a big payout. Pentesters have 2 weeks and move on. The most creative exploits come from bounty hunters.
  • Noise management is real: Bug bounty programs generate hundreds of low-quality reports. Triaging "I found an open redirect" submissions is a full-time job.
  • Do both: Annual pentests for compliance and systematic coverage. Continuous bug bounty for ongoing discovery. They complement each other — they're not substitutes.
