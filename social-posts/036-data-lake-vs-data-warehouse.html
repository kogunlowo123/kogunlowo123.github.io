The Real Trade-offs

  • Data lakes on S3 cost $0.023/GB — 10x cheaper than Redshift storage. But without Glue Crawlers and governance, data becomes unfindable.
  • BigQuery blurs the line: it's a warehouse that queries external data lake files. Pay per query eliminates idle cluster costs.
  • Azure Synapse tried to unify both — dedicated SQL pools for warehouse workloads, serverless pools for lake queries. But managing both is double the operational burden.
  • The lakehouse approach with Athena + Apache Iceberg gives you ACID transactions on your data lake — closing the biggest gap between lakes and warehouses.
  • Data warehouses enforce schema-on-write; data lakes allow schema-on-read. If your analysts need sub-second dashboards, the warehouse's columnar storage wins every time.
  • For most orgs: structured business data goes in the warehouse, unstructured logs and ML training data go in the lake. Trying to put everything in one place is where architectures fail.
