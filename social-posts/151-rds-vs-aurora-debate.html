The Real Trade-offs

  • Cost premium is real: Aurora typically costs 20-30% more per instance hour plus I/O charges that can spike unpredictably. For predictable OLTP workloads, standard RDS Multi-AZ is often the better value.
  • Failover is genuinely faster: Aurora's shared storage means replicas don't need to replay logs — failover takes ~30 seconds vs. 60-120 seconds for RDS Multi-AZ. If that difference matters, Aurora earns its premium.
  • I/O pricing is the hidden trap: Aurora charges per million I/O requests. High-throughput workloads can see storage costs balloon. RDS includes I/O in the instance price with provisioned IOPS.
  • 15 replicas vs. 5 matter at scale: If you need more than 5 read replicas or instant failover between them, Aurora is genuinely superior. Most teams never reach that scale.
  • Aurora Serverless v2 is the real differentiator: If your workload is bursty, Serverless v2's ACU-based scaling is something standard RDS simply cannot match. This alone can justify Aurora for the right use case.
