BigQuery's Pricing Model Is the Most Honest

Snowflake's credit system is deliberately opaque — try predicting your bill without a PhD in warehouse sizing. Redshift makes you pay for idle clusters. BigQuery charges per TB scanned and separates storage from compute. You pay for what you use. Period.

The Real Trade-offs

  • Snowflake credits are intentionally confusing: Credit consumption varies by warehouse size, cloud provider, and region. The sales team loves this complexity because it requires engagement to understand your bill.
  • BigQuery per-TB can be expensive at scale: On-demand pricing ($6.25/TB) is honest but expensive for heavy analytical workloads. Flat-rate slots become cheaper at scale — but that's back to reserved capacity.
  • Redshift Serverless closed the gap: RPU-based billing means you no longer pay for idle provisioned clusters. But RPU pricing can still surprise you with complex queries.
  • Multi-cloud is Snowflake's real moat: If you run workloads across AWS, Azure, and GCP, Snowflake is the only option that works identically everywhere. That flexibility has real value.
  • Total cost includes data movement: Egress charges, cross-region fees, and data staging costs can dwarf compute costs. The cheapest warehouse is the one closest to your data.
