You spent $2M on VMware licenses, hired 5 infrastructure engineers, and called it "private cloud." You still can't spin up a VM in under 2 weeks. Meanwhile, AWS provisions compute in 30 seconds. Private cloud is on-prem with a marketing budget.

The Real Trade-offs

  • AWS gives you 200+ managed services — private cloud gives you VMs and storage, maybe
  • VMware licensing costs + hardware refresh cycles make private cloud total cost equal or higher than public
  • Data sovereignty and regulatory requirements are the only valid reasons for private cloud
  • AWS Outposts bridges the gap — run AWS APIs on your hardware
  • At extreme scale (10,000+ servers), private cloud economics start to make sense — but you're not at that scale
