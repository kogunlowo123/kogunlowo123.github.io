Backstage Is Over-Engineered for Most Organizations

Spotify built Backstage for 2000+ engineers across hundreds of services. You have 30 engineers and 15 services. Setting up Backstage will take your platform team 3 months and require ongoing maintenance of a React app, a PostgreSQL database, and a plugin ecosystem. Is that really simpler than a spreadsheet?

The Real Trade-offs

  • Backstage is a framework, not a product: Out of the box, Backstage does very little. You need to build plugins, configure integrations, and maintain it like any other internal application. Budget 1-2 full-time engineers.
  • Port and managed alternatives save time: SaaS platforms like Port, Cortex, and OpsLevel give you 80% of Backstage's value with 20% of the setup effort. Trade customization for speed.
  • Custom portals become legacy fast: Every custom-built IDP eventually becomes the thing nobody wants to maintain. At least Backstage has a community and upgrade path.
  • The right size matters: Under 50 engineers? A well-maintained README per repo, a Notion wiki, and standardized CI templates might be your IDP. Don't build infrastructure for problems you don't have.
  • Backstage shines at scale: Above 200 engineers, the discoverability problem is real. "Who owns this service? Where are the docs? How do I deploy?" Backstage answers these questions in one place.
