MLflow vs SageMaker Experiments — Open Source vs Cloud-Native ML Tracking

MLflow is the de facto standard for experiment tracking — open source, cloud-agnostic, backed by Databricks. SageMaker Experiments is tightly woven into AWS with automatic capture from training jobs, endpoints, and pipelines. The portability vs. integration trade-off defines this debate. Your cloud commitment level is the real decision variable.

The Real Trade-offs

  • MLflow's portability is its superpower: Run it locally during development, on Databricks in production, or self-hosted on any cloud. Your experiment logs, model artifacts, and registry are not locked to a vendor. For multi-cloud or hybrid teams, this is non-negotiable.
  • SageMaker Experiments auto-captures everything: Training job hyperparameters, input datasets, model artifacts, and endpoint metrics are logged automatically when using SageMaker training jobs. No manual mlflow.log_param() calls needed. This reduces friction for teams already on SageMaker.
  • MLflow's community ecosystem is broader: Integrations with PyTorch, TensorFlow, XGBoost, scikit-learn, Hugging Face — plus community plugins for custom flavors. SageMaker Experiments works best within the SageMaker ecosystem and feels bolted-on outside of it.
  • Self-hosting MLflow has operational cost: The tracking server needs a database (PostgreSQL/MySQL), artifact storage (S3/GCS), and authentication. Databricks Managed MLflow eliminates this but adds vendor dependency. SageMaker Experiments is serverless with no infrastructure to manage.
  • The best answer is often MLflow on your cloud: Use MLflow as the tracking API (it's framework-agnostic) but deploy it on your primary cloud — Databricks Managed MLflow on AWS/Azure/GCP, or self-hosted MLflow with your cloud's storage backend. You get portability without operational pain.
