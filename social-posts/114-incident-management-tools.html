The Real Trade-offs

  • Process beats tools every time: A team with a spreadsheet and a clear escalation matrix will outperform a team with PagerDuty Enterprise and no incident commander role defined.
  • Tool integrations do matter: PagerDuty's event intelligence, Slack integration, and analytics help mature teams. But those features are useless without foundational processes.
  • Opsgenie is cheaper and "good enough": For teams already in the Atlassian ecosystem, Opsgenie's Jira integration is a natural fit. PagerDuty is better standalone but at 3-5x the cost.
  • New entrants focus on experience: incident.io and Rootly emphasize Slack-native workflows and automation. They reduce context switching during incidents, which genuinely reduces MTTR.
  • The real investment is practice: Run regular incident drills. Review every postmortem for action items. Track MTTR trends. No tool does this for you — it's a cultural commitment.
