The Real Trade-offs

  • ASL is painful at scale: A 50-state workflow in JSON is unmaintainable. Temporal lets you write the same logic in 200 lines of Go with full IDE support, type safety, and unit testing.
  • Step Functions has zero vendor lock-in escape: ASL is AWS-only. Temporal runs anywhere — AWS, GCP, on-prem. Your workflow code is portable.
  • Step Functions wins on zero-ops: No clusters to manage, no workers to scale. For simple 5-10 step workflows, the managed service is genuinely easier.
  • Temporal's replay model is superior: Deterministic replay means you can debug any workflow execution step-by-step. Step Functions gives you a visual graph and CloudWatch logs.
  • Cost at scale favors Temporal: Step Functions charges per state transition ($0.025/1000). A workflow with 20 states running 1M times/month = $500. Temporal on EKS is a flat infrastructure cost.
